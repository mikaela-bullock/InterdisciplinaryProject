Interdisciplinary Project: Art & Computer Science

What is the relationship between STEM and Humanities thinking and expertise?

About the Creator

My name is Mikaela Bullock, and I am a student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison majoring in Economics (Math Emphasis) and Computer Science.

I care deeply about the question this project asks because I live at the intersection of technical and humanistic thinking. As someone trained in programming, data analysis, and mathematical modeling, I also spend significant time writing, teaching, and communicating complex ideas to others.

This project emerges from my belief that technical expertise alone is not enough. Meaningful work in STEM requires interpretation, narrative, and ethical reflection— skills traditionally associated with the humanities. I am interested in how these modes of thinking inform and strengthen one another rather than existing in opposition.

Project Overview

This website serves as both my project and my argument: technical skills and humanistic expression are deeply interconnected. Through a digital medium built with HTML and CSS, I demonstrate how coding and communication work together to convey meaning.

By using the structure of a website as my medium, I highlight how even technical tools rely on rhetorical choices—organization, emphasis, and visual design—to guide interpretation.

Selected Artifact

My project draws on the Art of Science exhibition as an example of how visual media bridges scientific inquiry and artistic interpretation. The exhibition illustrates how scientific data, experimentation, and observation can be transformed into visual forms that invite reflection and emotional engagement.

Argument & Reflection

This project argues that technical knowledge becomes meaningful only when paired with the ability to articulate, interpret, and communicate ideas. Writing, analysis, and critical thinking—skills rooted in the humanities—shape how information is presented and understood, even in digital and computational environments.

In creating this website, I rely on coding as the technical foundation, while my humanities background guides the narrative structure, tone, and purpose. The final product reflects my belief that interdisciplinary thinking is essential to both effective communication and responsible technological work.
